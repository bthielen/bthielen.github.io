the interface

homepage

I picked a clean hero image of a wood counter that left enough whitespace for design elements but included food items around the edges to support the site's theme. I kept the site's header an extension of the wooden counter with vegetables to carry the theme across all 3 pages. I designed the CSS so that the hero image wouldn't scale with the page but its edges would trim from both sides, keeping the cutting board centered. I used black and white elements with lowered opacity for a modern feel that allowed the appetizing images to show through. I changed the site description's typeface to Open Sans in response to class feedback that the original typeface, Bebas Neue, was too hard to read in a block of text.

recipes

I incorporated the steele gray color prominantly on the recipes page, using it to make the controls stand out from the rest of the page. I kept the buttons consistent across the website, using a persimmon background color and a white stroke to make them pop against whitespace and a steele background, insuring that users would notice the 'add ingredients' button.

about us/contact

I used the image of two people cooking together as a backdrop for "Passionate Folks Who Love Food" to visually reinforce the tagline, using an image with people that fit the company description of "a team of college students". I kept the company description in a grey, 12pt typeface that took up equivalent space to its neighbors, visually balancing the layout.
