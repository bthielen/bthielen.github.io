SolarSPELL

Design Challenge

Redesign a global leader in crowdsourcing's spinsoff product to increase conversions. The product is a crowdsourcing platform that big-time companies use to sponsor humanitarian challenges.

The client requested that I redesign the challenge participant's user flow to increase simplicity, to ultimately result in more engagement and conversions.

My Role

I led the redesign of my marketing agency's client's website, as the primary UX designer. I spearheaded the direction of the project and the work we proposed to the client. I was in the charge of the UX side of the project and helped mentor our intern who designed the UI.

The Original Site

All Challenges

  • No universal search bar present

  • The navigation is contained inside of a 3-bar hamburger menu and only appears on the sidebar when clicked

  • Navigation contains 11 links, 3 of which lead to the same page.

  • Filtering and sorting is hidden inside of a button instead of being readily available

Registration

  • User has to click through and accept 4 pages of terms and conditions

  • There is no breadcrumb navigation or progress bar indicating when the user is done registering

  • If user selects "Yes I Want To Join A Team," they are taken to a mandatory "Tell Others About Yourself" form which the user is forced to complete before being able to view the teams.

  • Overall, the registration process is tiring with 10+ pages to click through and the steps are unintuitive as they do not seem related and show no end in sight

Personas

My target user group is university students and young professionals, especially those in STEM career paths.

User Flow

I redesigned the challenge participant's user flow with the user's point of entry in mind, designing wireframes to flow according to the context of the client's marketing plans which included social ads and organic traffic.

I consolidated stray pages into a simplifed, cohesive flow, breaking down the user's journey down into steps that were labeled with a clear purpose and linked together in logical order.
